Trump Authorized Covert CIA Action in Venezuela, WaPo Reveals

Washington, D.C. – Former President Donald Trump authorized covert CIA operations in Venezuela, according to a bombshell report by The Washington Post. The secret directive, signed early in his presidency, empowered the CIA to conduct clandestine activities aimed at pressuring or even overthrowing Nicolás Maduro's government.

The previously undisclosed authorization highlights the Trump administration's aggressive, behind-the-scenes strategy to unseat Maduro. While the U.S. publicly imposed sanctions and supported opposition leaders, these covert actions represented a deeper, unadvertised intervention. Despite significant U.S. efforts, including these secret operations, Maduro has retained power, a testament to the challenges of regime change. Details of the directive remained highly classified until now, offering new insights into U.S. foreign policy tactics in Latin America.
