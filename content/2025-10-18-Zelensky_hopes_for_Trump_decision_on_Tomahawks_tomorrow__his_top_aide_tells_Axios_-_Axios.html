Zelensky Anticipates Trump's Tomahawk Decision, Top Aide Tells Axios

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky is reportedly holding out hope for a swift decision from Donald Trump regarding the provision of long-range Tomahawk missiles to Ukraine, potentially as early as tomorrow. This development comes from a top aide who spoke with Axios.

These advanced cruise missiles are seen as vital for bolstering Ukraine's defense, offering crucial extended strike capabilities against Russian targets. Zelensky's proactive anticipation highlights the critical nature of ongoing military aid discussions and the significant influence Trump's stance could hold over future support for Kyiv. The potential delivery of Tomahawks remains a key topic in international defense talks.
